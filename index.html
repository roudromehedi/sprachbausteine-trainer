No minus points. Always guess. In Teil 1 you have 33% chance even guessing randomly!

Rule 2: You only need 60% (= 12 out of 20 correct)

That's just 6/10 per Teil. You do NOT need perfection.
🇧🇩 মাত্র ৬০% লাগে (২০-এর মধ্যে ১২টা)। Perfect হতে হবে না!

Stuck? Mark best guess, come back later. Don't lose easy points elsewhere.

✏️ Teil 1: 3 Tricks That Solve Most Gaps

Trick 1: VERB POSITION (solves 3-4 gaps per exam!)

Where is the verb in the clause after the gap?

🔵 Verb at the END → Nebensatz connector:
weil, dass, ob, wenn, als, obwohl, damit, sobald

🟢 Verb right after gap → Adverb connector:
deshalb, trotzdem, außerdem, dann

🟡 Verb in position 2 (normal) →
und, aber, oder, denn

🇧🇩 Gap-এর পর verb কোথায়?
🔵 Verb শেষে → weil/dass/ob/wenn/obwohl
🟢 Verb gap-এর ঠিক পরে → deshalb/trotzdem
🟡 Verb স্বাভাবিক জায়গায় → und/aber/oder/denn

Trick 2: DATIV DETECTION (solves pronoun gaps)

Options have mir/mich or dir/dich? Check for Dativ triggers:

✅ Dativ verbs: gefallen, helfen, schreiben, geben, schicken
✅ Dativ prepositions: mit, bei, zu, von, seit, nach, aus

Trigger found → pick mir/dir
No trigger → pick mich/dich

🇧🇩 mir/mich বা dir/dich দেখলে চেক করো: Dativ verb আছে (gefallen/helfen/schreiben)? বা Dativ preposition আছে (mit/bei/zu)? থাকলে → mir/dir। না থাকলে → mich/dich।

Trick 3: DEGREE FORMS (-er = als, so = wie, am = -sten)

• See "-er" form → answer is als
• See "so..._" → answer is wie + Grundform
• See "am _" → pick the -sten form
• See "_ genug" → always Grundform

🇧🇩 • Comparative (-er) দেখলে → als
• "so...___" দেখলে → wie + মূল রূপ
• "am ___" দেখলে → -sten form
• "___ genug" → সবসময় মূল রূপ

📝 Teil 2: 4 Tricks for the Word Bank

Trick 1: 3-Round Method

Round 1: Fill the obvious ones (fixed phrases, clear grammar)
Round 2: Cross out used words, fewer options now
Round 3: Match remaining words by TYPE

🇧🇩 ৩ রাউন্ড পদ্ধতি:
১) প্রথমে সহজগুলো পূরণ করো
২) ব্যবহৃত শব্দ কেটে দাও
৩) বাকিগুলোর word type মিলাও

Trick 2: Word Type Detection

• Gap + noun → Article/Possessive (IHR, UNSEREN, EINEN)
• Gap between sentences → Connector (DESHALB, DENN, ABER)
• "sich/ich" + gap + Infinitiv → Modal (MÖCHTE, WÜRDE, KÖNNTE)
• Verb + "über/auf" → dar-form (DARÜBER, DAFÜR)
• Nothing grammar-related → Adverb (AUCH, GERN, NUR, DORT)

🇧🇩 Gap-এর আশপাশ দেখো কী ধরনের শব্দ লাগবে:
• Gap + noun → Article (IHR, EINEN)
• দুই বাক্যের মাঝে → Connector (DESHALB, DENN)
• কোনো grammar দরকার নেই → Adverb (AUCH, GERN, NUR)

Trick 3: Fixed Phrases = FREE Points

From your actual exams:
• "AUF Ihre Antwort freue ich mich"
• "VOM 1. bis zum 14. September"
• "IM Internet gelesen"
• "informieren Sie uns DARÜBER"
• "DESHALB schreibe ich diese E-Mail"

🇧🇩 এই phrases গুলো মুখস্থ করো — প্রতি exam-এ ২-৩টা free marks!

Trick 4: Adverbs Fill the Last Gaps

After grammar gaps are done, leftover words like AUCH, GERN, SCHON, NUR, DORT, GANZ just complete the meaning. No grammar needed — just ask "what makes sense here?"

🇧🇩 Grammar gap-গুলো করার পর বাকি শব্দগুলো (AUCH, GERN, NUR, DORT) শুধু অর্থ মেলাও! Grammar লাগে না — শুধু ভাবো "কোনটা অর্থ করে?"

🧠 Exam Day: Quick Decision Flowchart

For every gap, ask in this order:

1. Formal or informal? (Sehr geehrte = Sie/Ihnen | Liebe = du/dir)
2. Where is the verb? (End = weil/dass/ob | After gap = deshalb | Normal = denn/aber)
3. Dativ trigger? (mit/bei/zu + helfen/gefallen → mir/dir)
4. Comparative? (-er = als | so...= wie | am = -sten)
5. Verb needs preposition? (freuen auf → darauf)
6. None of above? → Simple meaning-word (adverb)

🇧🇩 প্রতিটা gap-এ এই ক্রমে ভাবো:
১. Formal নাকি informal?
২. Verb কোথায়? (শেষে/gap-এর পরে/স্বাভাবিক)
৩. Dativ trigger আছে?
৪. Comparative আছে?
৫. Verb+Preposition combo?
৬. কিছু না → সাধারণ adverb!

Last resort: Read it in your head!

Your ear often "knows" the right answer. If one option sounds natural → trust it. Never change your first answer unless you find a clear grammar reason.

🇧🇩 শেষ উপায়: মনে মনে পড়ো! তোমার কান প্রায়ই সঠিক উত্তর "জানে"। যেটা স্বাভাবিক শোনায় সেটাই দাও। প্রথম উত্তর পাল্টাবে না — শুধু clear grammar reason থাকলে!

⚡ TEIL 1: The 11 Patterns (80% of all gaps!)

Master these 11 things and Teil 1 is basically solved. They cover the vast majority of what appears.

Pattern 1: Articles + Case (THE most common!)

💡 You choose between der/die/das, ein/eine/einen, kein/keine. The answer depends on CASE.

What triggers the case:
  • DATIV → after: mit, bei, zu, von, seit, nach, aus
    🇧🇩 এগুলোর পর সবসময় Dativ (mir/dir/dem/der)
  • AKKUSATIV → after: für, ohne, um, durch, gegen
    🇧🇩 এগুলোর পর সবসময় Akkusativ (mich/dich/den/die)
  • BOTH → after: in, an, auf — Dativ (where?) or Akkusativ (where to?)
    🇧🇩 কোথায় আছি? = Dativ | কোথায় যাচ্ছি? = Akkusativ
Example:

"Ich möchte mich bei ____ Nachbarin bedanken."
a) meine   b) meiner   c) meinem
→ b) meiner — "bei" takes Dativ, "Nachbarin" is feminine → meiner

🎯 Shortcut:

After einen/einem/einer/eines, the adjective ending is ALMOST ALWAYS -en. This one rule covers huge numbers of cases!

Pattern 2: Time & Place Prepositions

💡 These show up CONSTANTLY because telc texts describe events, plans, schedules.

PrepositionUseExample
seit + Datduration, STILL ongoingseit zwei Jahren
vor + Datago, FINISHEDvor einem Monat

amdays & datesam Montag, am 5. Mai

🎯 The seit vs. vor trap (appears in EVERY exam):

seit = started in past, STILL true now: "Ich wohne seit 3 Jahren hier" (still living here)
vor = completed in the past: "Ich bin vor 3 Jahren umgezogen" (move is done)

🇧🇩 seit = এখনো চলছে ("৩ বছর ধরে এখানে থাকি") | vor = শেষ হয়ে গেছে ("৩ বছর আগে চলে এসেছি")

Pattern 3: Verb Forms – Perfekt, Präteritum, Modals

💡 They test: do you know the right tense and the right form?

haben vs. sein in Perfekt:

Most verbs use haben. But MOVEMENT & CHANGE verbs use sein:
gehen, fahren, fliegen, kommen, laufen, reisen, umziehen, aufstehen, einschlafen, werden, bleiben, passieren, sterben

"Wir sind nach Spanien gefahren." (NOT haben!)
"Ich habe ein Buch gelesen." (no movement)

Modal verbs in past (Präteritum = more common in writing):

konnte, musste, durfte, sollte, wollte, mochte
"Ich konnte leider nicht kommen."

Konjunktiv II (polite requests — appears in EVERY formal email!):

würde, könnte, hätte, wäre, müsste, dürfte
"Könnten Sie mir bitte...?" / "Ich würde gern..." / "Es wäre schön, wenn..."

🎯 If text starts with "Sehr geehrte..." → expect Konjunktiv II for requests!

Pattern 4: Connectors & Word Order (THE trick that solves 3-4 gaps)

💡 Just look at WHERE THE VERB IS. That's your answer.

Group A — Verb stays normal (position 2):

und, aber, oder, denn, sondern
"Ich komme nicht, denn ich bin krank."

Group B — Verb jumps to position 1 (inverted):

deshalb, trotzdem, dann, danach, deswegen, außerdem
"Ich bin krank. Deshalb bleibe ich zu Hause."

Group C — Verb goes to the END (Nebensatz):

weil, dass, ob, wenn, als, obwohl, bevor, nachdem, damit, bis, sobald, sodass, falls
"Ich bleibe zu Hause, weil ich krank bin."

🎯 THE TRICK: Look at where the verb IS, then pick the connector!

• Verb at the END of the clause → you need Group C
• Verb immediately after the gap → you need Group B
• Verb in normal position 2 → you need Group A

This one trick alone solves 3-4 gaps in every exam!

🇧🇩 Verb শেষে → Group C (weil/dass/ob/wenn)। Verb gap-এর ঠিক পরে → Group B (deshalb/trotzdem)। Verb স্বাভাবিক → Group A (und/aber/denn)। এই একটা trick-এ প্রতি exam-এ ৩-৪টা gap solve হয়!

💡 Check: Is there a Dativ verb or a Dativ preposition? → Dativ. Otherwise → Akkusativ.

🎯 Memorize these DATIV verbs (always Dativ!):

helfen, gefallen, gehören, danken, gratulieren, schmecken, passen, Bescheid geben/sagen
"Das gefällt mir." / "Kannst du mir helfen?" / "Gib mir Bescheid."

Pattern 6: Adjective Endings

💡 One shortcut covers 80% of cases!

THE SHORTCUT:

After einen/einem/einer/eines → adjective ending is almost ALWAYS -en
"einen interessanten Artikel" / "einem netten Mann" / "einer guten Idee"

Common exam phrases:

• "mit freundlichen Grüßen" (Dativ plural)
• "im nächsten Jahr" (Dativ neutral/masc)
• "einen neuen Job" (Akk maskulin)

Pattern 7: Negation – kein vs. nicht (rare in exam!)

💡 Rarely tested directly in Sprachbausteine, but good to know:

kein = replaces "ein" (negates NOUNS)

"Ich habe ein Auto" → "Ich habe kein Auto."
"Wir haben keine Zimmer mehr frei."

nicht = negates everything else (verbs, adjectives, adverbs)

"Ich komme nicht." / "Das ist nicht richtig."

Pattern 8: Degree Forms – als/wie + Superlatives

💡 Tested in 3 out of 4 Modeltests! Three simple rules:

Rule 1: Comparative (-er) → always ALS

"Berlin ist größer ALS München." / "weniger ALS vorher"
🎯 See -er form? → answer is als, NEVER "wie"!

Rule 2: so...WIE = equal (Grundform only!)

"nicht so schlimm, wie erwartet" (NOT schlimmer!)
After "so...wie" → always GRUNDFORM (no -er, no -sten)

Rule 3: am ___STEN = Superlative

"Geld ist nicht am wichtigsten" / "am besten" / "am liebsten"
🎯 See "am ___" with choices like wichtig/wichtiger/wichtigsten → pick the -sten one!

Rule 4: ___ + genug = always Grundform

"groß genug" (NOT größer genug!)

Pattern 9: Konjunktiv II – Polite Requests

💡 Appears in EVERY formal email/letter!

Key forms to recognize:

würde, könnte, hätte, wäre, müsste, dürfte

"Könnten Sie mir bitte die Unterlagen schicken?"
"Ich würde gern einen Termin vereinbaren."
"Es wäre schön, wenn Sie..."

🎯 Exam Trap: können vs. könnten vs. konnten

• können = can (present, neutral)
• könnten = could (polite request) ← CORRECT in formal emails!
• konnten = could (past tense, not polite)

Formal email + request? → Always könnten!

Pattern 10: Infinitiv mit "zu" (Trennbare Verben trap!)

💡 After these words → ALWAYS "zu + Infinitiv":

Trigger words:

Lust haben, versuchen, anfangen, aufhören, vergessen, planen, hoffen, es ist möglich/wichtig/schön

Normal verb → zu + verb:

"Ich habe vergessen, das Fenster zu schließen."

Trennbare verb → prefix + zu + verb (ONE word!):

• mit|kommen → mitzukommen
• auf|stehen → aufzustehen
• mit|bringen → mitzubringen
• an|fangen → anzufangen

🎯 THE TRAP (appears every exam!):

Options: a) mitgekommen   b) mitkommen   c) mitzukommen
If you see "Lust / versuchen / möglich / anfangen" before the gap → answer is ALWAYS the "...zu..." version (c)!

Also: um...zu = "in order to" (same subject): "Ich lerne, um die Prüfung zu bestehen."
damit = "so that" (different subjects): "Ich lerne, damit mein Chef zufrieden ist."

⚠️ DAMIT has 2 meanings! "damit" = so that (connector, verb at end) OR "damit" = with that (pronominal adverb, da+mit). Context tells you which!

🇧🇩 Lust/versuchen/möglich দেখলেই "zu" version বাছো! um...zu = নিজে করতে (একই subject)। damit = অন্যে করবে (আলাদা subject)। সতর্ক: damit দুই অর্থে আসে — "যাতে" (connector) বা "সেটা দিয়ে" (da+mit)!

Pattern 11: Relative Clauses (der/die/das after a comma)

💡 2 steps: 1) Find the noun → gives you gender. 2) Check role in clause → gives you case.

NominativAkkusativDativ
maskulin (der)derdendem
feminin (die)diedieder
neutral (das)dasdasdem
Plural (die)diediedenen

🎯 Quick method:

1. Find the noun before the comma → that's the gender
2. Does the relative clause already have a subject (ich/du/er/man...)?
• NO subject → you need Nominativ (der/die/das)
• YES has subject → you need Akkusativ (den/die/das) or Dativ

"Das Angebot, ___ günstig ist" → no subject → das (Nom)
"Die Firma, bei ___ ich arbeite" → "ich" is subject, "bei" = Dativ → der (Dat fem)

🇧🇩 ১. কমার আগের noun-এর gender দেখো। ২. Relative clause-এ subject আছে কি? নেই → Nominativ (der/die/das)। আছে → Akkusativ বা Dativ।

Pattern 14: Logical Connectors (Sequence & Contrast)

💡 Read the sentence BEFORE and AFTER the gap. The logic tells you which connector fits.

Choose based on LOGIC:
  • Result/consequence → deshalb, deswegen, daher
  • Contradiction/contrast → trotzdem, allerdings, aber
  • Addition (more info) → außerdem, auch
  • Alternative → oder
  • Sequence → zuerst, dann, danach, schließlich
Example from your exam:

"Wir würden ABER gern zwei Wochen bleiben" → contrast (7 nights is good, BUT we want 2 weeks)
"Ich möchte AUßERDEM wissen" → addition (another question on top)

Pattern 16: Common Adverbs & Particles (Teil 2!)

💡 2-3 gaps in every Teil 2 are simple adverbs. They test MEANING, not grammar!

When to suspect an adverb:

If the gap doesn't need a grammar word (no connector, no preposition, no pronoun) → it's a meaning-adverb.

🎯 Teil 2 Trick:

Fill grammar gaps first (prepositions, connectors, pronouns). Then the leftover simple adverbs fit the remaining "meaning" gaps easily!

🔥 BONUS: The Confusing Pairs They LOVE to Test

These pairs trip up most students. Know the difference = easy points.

PairRule

weil vs. dennboth = because | weil = verb END | denn = verb normal
trotzdem vs. obwohlboth = although | trotzdem = verb next | obwohl = verb END
um...zu vs. damitboth = in order to | um...zu = same subject | damit = different subjects
als vs. wieals = after comparative (-er) | wie = after "so" (equal)
noch vs. schonnoch = still | schon = already
kein vs. nichtkein = negates nouns (replaces ein) | nicht = negates verbs/adj

🏆 THE ULTIMATE 5-STEP METHOD (Use on Exam Day)

Step 1: Read the whole text (30 sec)

What's it about? Formal or informal? Who writes to whom?

Step 2: For each gap — read the FULL sentence

Not just the gap. Read from comma to comma or period to period.

Step 3: Identify the CATEGORY

Is this testing a preposition? connector? pronoun? verb form? Naming it narrows your thinking immediately.

Step 4: Use the VERB POSITION trick

Where is the verb? → That tells you which connector/conjunction fits.

Step 5: Eliminate, then choose

Cross out the clearly wrong option. Now you have 50/50. Trust your gut. NEVER leave it empty!
