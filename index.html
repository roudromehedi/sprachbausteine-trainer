⚡ The Golden Rules

Rule 1: NEVER leave a gap empty!

No minus points. Always guess. In Teil 1 you have 33% chance even guessing randomly!
🇧🇩 কখনো খালি রেখো না! কোনো minus point নেই। Teil 1-এ random guess-এও ৩৩% chance!

Rule 2: You only need 60% (= 12 out of 20 correct)

That's just 6/10 per Teil. You do NOT need perfection.
🇧🇩 মাত্র ৬০% লাগে (২০-এর মধ্যে ১২টা)। Perfect হতে হবে না!

Rule 3: Max 40 seconds per gap — then move on

Stuck? Mark best guess, come back later. Don't lose easy points elsewhere.
🇧🇩 একটা gap-এ ৪০ সেকেন্ডের বেশি না! আটকে গেলে best guess দিয়ে পরেরটায় যাও।

Rule 4: Just 2 patterns cover 30% of ALL gaps!

Connectors + Verb Forms alone = 24 out of 80 answers. Learn these two well → already halfway to passing.
🇧🇩 শুধু Connectors + Verb Forms = ৩০% উত্তর! এই দুটো ভালো করলেই অর্ধেক পাস!

✏️ Teil 1: 3 Tricks That Solve Most Gaps

Trick 1: VERB POSITION (solves 3-4 gaps per exam!)

Where is the verb in the clause after the gap?

🔵 Verb at the END → Nebensatz connector:
weil, dass, ob, wenn, als, obwohl, damit, sobald

🟢 Verb right after gap → Adverb connector:
deshalb, trotzdem, außerdem, dann

🟡 Verb in position 2 (normal) →
und, aber, oder, denn

🇧🇩 Gap-এর পর verb কোথায়?
🔵 Verb শেষে → weil/dass/ob/wenn/obwohl
🟢 Verb gap-এর ঠিক পরে → deshalb/trotzdem
🟡 Verb স্বাভাবিক জায়গায় → und/aber/oder/denn

Trick 2: DATIV DETECTION (solves pronoun gaps)

Options have mir/mich or dir/dich? Check for Dativ triggers:

✅ Dativ verbs: gefallen, helfen, schreiben, geben, schicken
✅ Dativ prepositions: mit, bei, zu, von, seit, nach, aus

Trigger found → pick mir/dir
No trigger → pick mich/dich

🇧🇩 mir/mich বা dir/dich দেখলে চেক করো: Dativ verb আছে (gefallen/helfen/schreiben)? বা Dativ preposition আছে (mit/bei/zu)? থাকলে → mir/dir। না থাকলে → mich/dich।

Trick 3: DEGREE FORMS (-er = als, so = wie, am = -sten)

• See "-er" form → answer is als
• See "so..._" → answer is wie + Grundform
• See "am _" → pick the -sten form
• See "_ genug" → always Grundform

🇧🇩 • Comparative (-er) দেখলে → als
• "so...___" দেখলে → wie + মূল রূপ
• "am ___" দেখলে → -sten form
• "___ genug" → সবসময় মূল রূপ

📝 Teil 2: 4 Tricks for the Word Bank

Trick 1: 3-Round Method

Round 1: Fill the obvious ones (fixed phrases, clear grammar)
Round 2: Cross out used words, fewer options now
Round 3: Match remaining words by TYPE

🇧🇩 ৩ রাউন্ড পদ্ধতি:
১) প্রথমে সহজগুলো পূরণ করো
২) ব্যবহৃত শব্দ কেটে দাও
৩) বাকিগুলোর word type মিলাও

Trick 2: Word Type Detection

• Gap + noun → Article/Possessive (IHR, UNSEREN, EINEN)
• Gap between sentences → Connector (DESHALB, DENN, ABER)
• "sich/ich" + gap + Infinitiv → Modal (MÖCHTE, WÜRDE, KÖNNTE)
• Verb + "über/auf" → dar-form (DARÜBER, DAFÜR)
• Nothing grammar-related → Adverb (AUCH, GERN, NUR, DORT)

🇧🇩 Gap-এর আশপাশ দেখো কী ধরনের শব্দ লাগবে:
• Gap + noun → Article (IHR, EINEN)
• দুই বাক্যের মাঝে → Connector (DESHALB, DENN)
• কোনো grammar দরকার নেই → Adverb (AUCH, GERN, NUR)

Trick 3: Fixed Phrases = FREE Points

From your actual exams:
• "AUF Ihre Antwort freue ich mich"
• "VOM 1. bis zum 14. September"
• "IM Internet gelesen"
• "informieren Sie uns DARÜBER"
• "DESHALB schreibe ich diese E-Mail"

🇧🇩 এই phrases গুলো মুখস্থ করো — প্রতি exam-এ ২-৩টা free marks!

Trick 4: Adverbs Fill the Last Gaps

After grammar gaps are done, leftover words like AUCH, GERN, SCHON, NUR, DORT, GANZ just complete the meaning. No grammar needed — just ask "what makes sense here?"

🇧🇩 Grammar gap-গুলো করার পর বাকি শব্দগুলো (AUCH, GERN, NUR, DORT) শুধু অর্থ মেলাও! Grammar লাগে না — শুধু ভাবো "কোনটা অর্থ করে?"

🎓 Research-Backed Exam Hacks (from Prep Guides)

Sources: Heylama.com, U-Michigan German Grammar, HowToStudyGerman.com

Hack 1: Read the FULL sentence before looking at options

Your brain often "feels" the correct answer when you read the full context first. Looking at options too early creates confusion and second-guessing.

🇧🇩 Options দেখার আগে পুরো বাক্যটা পড়ো! তোমার brain প্রায়ই সঠিক উত্তর "অনুভব" করে যদি আগে context বোঝো।

Hack 2: Name the CATEGORY before choosing

Before picking an answer, identify: Is this gap testing an article? A preposition? A conjunction? A verb form? A pronoun? Naming the category narrows your thinking immediately.

🇧🇩 উত্তর দেওয়ার আগে ভাবো — এই gap কী test করছে? Article? Preposition? Conjunction? Category চিনলে চিন্তা সংকুচিত হয়!

Hack 3: "denn" can NEVER start a sentence!

"Denn" (because) is a coordinating conjunction — it connects two main clauses and can never begin a sentence. If you see a gap at the start of a sentence with "because" meaning → it must be "weil" or "da", not "denn".

Also: "weil/da" send the verb to the end; "denn" keeps normal word order (verb position 2).

🇧🇩 'denn' কখনো বাক্য শুরু করতে পারে না! বাক্যের শুরুতে "কারণ" বোঝাতে 'weil' বা 'da' লাগে। 'denn'-এর পর verb স্বাভাবিক, 'weil/da'-এর পর verb শেষে।

Hack 4: Signal words reveal the answer pattern

Certain words in the sentence "signal" what's needed in the gap:

• See "schon" or "bereits" → gap might need seit (ongoing duration)
• Movement verbs (gehen/fahren/fliegen) → Perfekt uses sein not haben
• "Sehr geehrte" → ALL blanks = formal Sie/Ihnen/Ihr
• Comma + verb at END → needs a subordinating conjunction
• "am" before a gap → superlative (-sten form)

🇧🇩 Signal words:
• "schon/bereits" → seit লাগতে পারে
• চলাচলের verb → sein Perfekt
• "Sehr geehrte" → formal (Sie/Ihnen)
• কমার পর verb শেষে → subordinating conjunction
• "am" + gap → superlative (-sten)

Hack 5: Use elimination — one option is ALWAYS clearly wrong

In Teil 1, one option is designed to be obviously wrong, one is "almost right" (the trap), and one is correct. Cross out the obvious wrong answer → now you have 50/50 odds.

In Teil 2, cross out already-placed words — each word is used ONCE. Fewer words remaining = easier final gaps.

🇧🇩 Teil 1-এ ৩টা option-এর ১টা সবসময় স্পষ্ট ভুল! কেটে দাও → ৫০/৫০ chance! Teil 2-তে ব্যবহৃত শব্দ কাটো → শেষ gap-গুলো সহজ হয়।

Hack 6: Conjunctional adverbs force verb to position 2

"Deshalb, trotzdem, deswegen, allerdings, dann, außerdem" — these occupy position 1 when opening a sentence, forcing the verb into position 2 and subject into position 3.

"Deshalb bleibe ich zu Hause." (verb pos.2, subject pos.3)
vs. "..., weil ich zu Hause bleibe." (subordinating = verb at END)

🇧🇩 deshalb/trotzdem = adverb → verb position 2-তে যায়: "Deshalb bleibe ich..." | weil/obwohl = verb শেষে!

Hack 7: Formal email = Konjunktiv II for ALL requests

Formal telc texts ALWAYS use Konjunktiv II (würde/könnte/hätte/wäre) for requests. If text starts with "Sehr geehrte..." → answer is polite form, NOT present tense.

Trap: "Können Sie..." (neutral) vs. "Könnten Sie..." (polite) — formal letters always want the polite form!

🇧🇩 Formal letter + request = Konjunktiv II। "Können" নয়, "Könnten" — formal email-এ সবসময় polite form!

Hack 8: "sowie" = "as well as" (list connector in Teil 2)

"Sowie" connects items in a list, meaning "as well as / and also". It often appears in Teil 2 word banks. Look for lists where normal "und" would sound repetitive.

"Kleidung, Bücher, sowie Geschirr" = clothes, books, as well as dishes.

🇧🇩 'sowie' = পাশাপাশি/এবং আরো। List-এ "und" ছাড়া item জোড়া লাগায়।

🧠 Exam Day: Quick Decision Flowchart

For every gap, ask in this order:

1. Formal or informal? (Sehr geehrte = Sie/Ihnen | Liebe = du/dir)
2. Where is the verb? (End = weil/dass/ob | After gap = deshalb | Normal = denn/aber)
3. Dativ trigger? (mit/bei/zu + helfen/gefallen → mir/dir)
4. Comparative? (-er = als | so...= wie | am = -sten)
5. Verb needs preposition? (freuen auf → darauf)
6. None of above? → Simple meaning-word (adverb)

🇧🇩 প্রতিটা gap-এ এই ক্রমে ভাবো:
১. Formal নাকি informal?
২. Verb কোথায়? (শেষে/gap-এর পরে/স্বাভাবিক)
৩. Dativ trigger আছে?
৪. Comparative আছে?
৫. Verb+Preposition combo?
৬. কিছু না → সাধারণ adverb!

Last resort: Read it in your head!

Your ear often "knows" the right answer. If one option sounds natural → trust it. Never change your first answer unless you find a clear grammar reason.

🇧🇩 শেষ উপায়: মনে মনে পড়ো! তোমার কান প্রায়ই সঠিক উত্তর "জানে"। যেটা স্বাভাবিক শোনায় সেটাই দাও। প্রথম উত্তর পাল্টাবে না — শুধু clear grammar reason থাকলে!

⚡ TEIL 1: The 11 Patterns

These 11 patterns cover 80% of all gaps. Each one has a simple "IF you see X → THEN answer is Y" rule.

🇧🇩 এই ১১টা pattern ৮০% gap cover করে। প্রতিটার একটা সহজ নিয়ম আছে: "X দেখলে → Y উত্তর দাও"।

1️⃣ Which form? (der/die/das, ein/eine/einen)

IF you see mit/bei/zu/von/seit/nach/aus → use DATIV form
IF you see für/ohne/um/durch/gegen → use AKKUSATIV form
🔵 These words ALWAYS need Dativ after them:

mit, bei, zu, von, seit, nach, aus

Think: "Mit, Von, Aus, Nach, Seit, Bei, Zu" = MR. VANS BuZ

🇧🇩 এই ৭টা শব্দের পর সবসময় Dativ: mit, bei, zu, von, seit, nach, aus
"MR. VANS BuZ" মনে রাখো!

🟡 These words ALWAYS need Akkusativ after them:

für, ohne, um, durch, gegen

Think: "Durch, Ohne, Gegen, Für" = DODGE-F

🇧🇩 এই ৫টার পর সবসময় Akkusativ: für, ohne, um, durch, gegen

📋 What does Dativ/Akkusativ actually look like?
AkkusativDativ
maskulin (der)den / einen / meinendem / einem / meinem
feminin (die)die / eine / meineder / einer / meiner
neutral (das)das / ein / meindem / einem / meinem
Plural (die)die / meineden / meinen
✅ Real exam example:

"mit ____ Eltern" → "mit" = Dativ, Plural → meinen
"für ____ E-Mail" → "für" = Akkusativ, E-Mail is feminine → deine

🇧🇩 "mit" দেখো → Dativ chart থেকে form বাছো। "für" দেখো → Akkusativ chart থেকে form বাছো। ব্যস!

2️⃣ Time words (seit/vor/am/im/um)

IF still happening now → seit | IF finished/ago → vor
📅 Which time word to use? Just match:
You see...Use...Example
Still true today ✓seit"Ich wohne seit 3 Jahren hier" (still here!)
Finished, done ✗vor"Vor 3 Jahren bin ich umgezogen" (move is done)
A day (Montag, Dienstag...)am"am Montag" / "am 5. Mai"
A month or seasonim"im Januar" / "im Sommer"
A clock timeum"um 8 Uhr"
Date range (from...to)vom...bis zum"vom 1. bis zum 14."
Starting from nowab"ab nächster Woche"

🎯 The #1 trap in every exam:

seit = I'm STILL doing it → "Ich lerne seit 2 Jahren Deutsch" (still learning!)
vor = I STOPPED → "Ich habe vor 2 Jahren angefangen" (that start is done)

🇧🇩 seit = এখনো করছি ("২ বছর ধরে") | vor = শেষ হয়ে গেছে ("২ বছর আগে")। প্রতি exam-এ আসে!

3️⃣ Verb forms (past tense, modals)

IF "haben/sein" + gap → use ge__t/ge__en (past participle)
IF modal verb (wollte/konnte) + gap → use basic form (Infinitiv)
🔑 Rule 1: After "haben" or "sein" → past participle (ge-form)

"Wir haben uns nicht gesehen." ← ge + seh + en
"Ich habe mich entschlossen." ← ent + schloss + en

🇧🇩 "haben/sein" + gap দেখলে → "ge" form দাও (gesehen, gefahren, geschrieben)

🔑 Rule 2: After modal verb → basic form (no ge-, no zu)

Modal verbs: wollte, konnte, musste, sollte, durfte, möchte

"Ich wollte dir schreiben." ← NOT "geschrieben"!
"Du wirst bestimmt eingeladen." ← werden + Partizip = passive

🇧🇩 Modal verb (wollte/konnte/musste) + gap → সবসময় basic form! "wollte schreiben" ✓ "wollte geschrieben" ✗

🔑 Rule 3: Movement verbs use "sein" (not "haben")

Going somewhere? → sein: "Wir sind gefahren / gegangen / geflogen / gekommen"
Doing something? → haben: "Ich habe gelesen / gekocht / geschrieben"

🇧🇩 যাওয়া/আসা/চলাচল = sein (sind gefahren)। অন্য সব = haben (habe gelesen)।

4️⃣ Connectors — THE trick that gives you 3-4 free answers!

Just look at WHERE THE VERB IS after the gap. That tells you the answer!
👀 Step 1: Find the verb after the gap

🔵 Verb at the END? → Use: weil, dass, ob, wenn, als, obwohl, damit, sobald
🟡 Verb RIGHT AFTER the gap? → Use: deshalb, trotzdem, außerdem, dann
🟠 Verb in normal place? → Use: und, aber, oder, denn

✅ See it in action:

🔵 "...__, du auch gern Spanisch lernen möchtest." → verb "möchtest" at END → dass

🟡 "Die Küche ist klein, __ können wir frühstücken." → verb "können" right after gap → trotzdem

🟠 "Ich ruf an, __ wir können sprechen." → subject between, verb normal → dann (wait, "dann" causes inversion too!)

🇧🇩 Verb কোথায় সেটাই তোমার উত্তর!
🔵 Verb একদম শেষে → weil/dass/ob/wenn
🟡 Verb gap-এর ঠিক পরে → deshalb/trotzdem
🟠 Verb স্বাভাবিক জায়গায় → und/aber/oder/denn

5️⃣ Pronouns: mir or mich? dir or dich?

IF you see helfen/gefallen/schreiben/geben OR mit/bei/zu → use mir/dir
IF none of those → use mich/dich
📋 The full chart (just 2 columns to learn!):

🔑 When to use mir/dir (Dativ):

After these verbs: helfen, gefallen, schreiben, geben, schicken, gehören
After these prepositions: mit, bei, zu, von, seit, nach, aus

"Kannst du mir helfen?" ← helfen = always mir/dir
"Fotos schicke ich dir" ← schicken = mir/dir (to whom?)
"mit dir zusammen" ← mit = always mir/dir

🇧🇩 helfen/gefallen/schreiben দেখলে → mir/dir
mit/bei/zu দেখলে → mir/dir
কিছু না দেখলে → mich/dich

6️⃣ Adjective endings (-en trick)

IF you see einem/meinem/deinem/seinem/ihrem before the adjective → ending is ALWAYS -en. Done!
The ONE rule that covers 80% of these questions:

After einem / meinem / deinem / seinem / ihrem / unserem
→ adjective ending is -en. Always. No exceptions in the exam!

✅ Examples from your exams:

"in meinem alten WG-Zimmer" ← meinem → -en
"im nächsten Jahr" ← im = in dem → -en
"mit freundlichen Grüßen" ← fixed phrase, always -en
"einen neuen Job" ← einen → -en

🇧🇩 einem/meinem/deinem দেখলে → adjective-এ -en লাগাও। ব্যস! এটুকুই মনে রাখো।

7️⃣ kein vs. nicht (rarely tested!)

IF negating a noun (replaces "ein") → kein | IF negating a verb/adjective → nicht
Simple rule:

• "Ich habe ein Auto" → "Ich habe kein Auto" (kein replaces ein)
• "Ich komme" → "Ich komme nicht" (nicht negates the verb)

🇧🇩 "ein" আছে → "kein" দিয়ে নেতিবাচক করো। verb/adjective → "nicht" দিয়ে। সোজা!

8️⃣ Comparing things (als/wie/am...sten)

See -er (bigger/smaller)? → als | See "so...___"? → wie | See "am ___"? → -sten form
Just 4 rules. That's it:

1️⃣ größer/kleiner/besser + ___? → always als (NEVER "wie"!)

2️⃣ so ___ wie? → use normal form: "so schlimm wie" (NOT schlimmer!)

3️⃣ am ___? → use -sten: "am wichtigsten / am besten"

4️⃣ ___ genug? → use normal form: "groß genug" (NOT größer genug!)

✅ From your exams:

"weniger als vorher" ← comparative → als
"nicht so schlimm, wie erwartet" ← so...wie → normal form
"nicht am wichtigsten" ← am → -sten form
"groß genug" ← genug → normal form

🇧🇩 -er দেখ → als | "so" দেখ → wie + মূল রূপ | "am" দেখ → -sten | "genug" দেখ → মূল রূপ

9️⃣ Polite forms (würde/könnte/hätte/wäre)

IF formal email + request/wish → answer is würde/könnte/hätte/wäre (NOT present tense!)
🔑 One simple rule:

See "Sehr geehrte..." (formal letter)? Any request or wish in that letter uses:

würde • könnte • hätte • wäre • müsste • dürfte

"Ich würde gern wissen..." = I would like to know
"Könnten Sie mir bitte...?" = Could you please...?
"Es wäre toll, wenn..." = It would be great if...

🇧🇩 Formal letter-এ polite request → সবসময় würde/könnte/hätte/wäre। "können" নয়, "könnten"!

⚠️ Common exam trap:

• können = can (normal, present) ← WRONG for formal requests
• könnten = could (polite) ← CORRECT for formal requests
• konnten = could (past tense, NOT polite) ← WRONG

🔟 "zu" + verb (the separable verb trap!)

IF you see Lust/versuchen/möglich/anfangen before gap → pick the "zu" version!
Separable verbs: write it as ONE word = mit-zu-kommen
🔑 These trigger words ALWAYS need "zu + verb" after them:

Lust haben, versuchen, anfangen, aufhören, vergessen, planen, hoffen, es ist möglich/wichtig/schön

✅ Normal verbs → zu + verb (two words):

"Ich habe vergessen, das Fenster zu schließen."

✅ Separable verbs → prefix + zu + verb (ONE word!):

mit|kommen → mitzukommen
mit|bringen → mitzubringen
an|fangen → anzufangen

✅ Exam example — spot the trap:

Options: a) mitgekommen   b) mitkommen   c) mitzukommen
"Hast du Lust, ___?" → "Lust" = trigger → answer is c) mitzukommen

🇧🇩 "Lust/versuchen/möglich" দেখলেই "zu" version দাও! Trennbare verb হলে একসাথে লেখো: mit+zu+kommen = mitzukommen

📋 Also remember: um...zu vs. damit

• um...zu = "in order to" (SAME person does both): "Ich lerne, um zu bestehen"
• damit = "so that" (DIFFERENT people): "Ich lerne, damit mein Chef zufrieden ist"

🇧🇩 um...zu = নিজেই করছি। damit = অন্য কেউ করবে।
